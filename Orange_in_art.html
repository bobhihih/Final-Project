Orange in Art

In the 18th century, orange was used to depict the robes of Pomona, the goddess of fruitful abundance. In 1797, French scientist Louis Vauquelin discovered the mineral crocoite, which led to the invention of pigment chrome orange in 1809.

Orange became an important colour for all the impressionist painters. After studying colour theory, they knew that orange placed next to azure blue would make both colours birghter.(source)
