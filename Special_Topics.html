Copyright Rules

Understanding copyright laws is crucial for web designers to avoid legal issues. Copyright protects original works of authorship, including websites and their content. It is important to use only content (images, text, music) that is licensed for use, or to create original content. Familiarize yourself with Creative Commons licenses, which allow creators to communicate which rights they reserve, and which they waive for the benefit of other creators.

Accessibility in Web Design

Accessibility is about making your website usable by as many people as possible, including those with disabilities. This means designing web content that can be used by people with visual, motor, auditory, speech, or cognitive disabilities. Implementing features like text-to-speech, keyboard navigation, and high-contrast visual design are crucial. Learn more from the CUNY Accessibility Guide.

Ethical Design

Ethical design considers the impact of design decisions on the well-being of users. It involves creating products that are not only functional and aesthetically pleasing but also socially responsible. This includes respecting user privacy, ensuring data protection, and fostering inclusivity. Ethical design avoids manipulative practices and strives for transparency in all aspects of user interaction. Learn more from the Ethical Design Guide
